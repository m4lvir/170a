The 170A Professional Roadmap

This path outlines the common training and certification milestones required for entry into and advancement within the 170A career field.

Phase 1: Foundational Knowledge & Entry

Objective: Establish baseline IT and security knowledge necessary for selection and put on path to becoming the Subject Matter Expert on a team.

Key Requirements:

  • **Knowledge:** Basic knowledge of coding and scripting lanaguages like C, Python, Powershell, and Bash.
  • **Experience:** Basic network troubleshooting and operating system administration.

Good Initial Certifications:

Certification Why it Matters Recertification Cycle
**CompTIA Security+** Meets DoD baseline requirements; validates core security concepts and compliance. 3 Years
**CompTIA Network+** Essential for understanding infrastructure, routing, and switching protocols. 3 Years

Phase 2: Core 170A Designation Training

Objective: Master the specific tools, methodologies, and doctrine required for the 170A role.

Core Training Modules:

  • **Module 2:** [Specific Module Name 2] (Advanced threat analysis).
  • **Field Training:** Minimum 6 months mentored experience in a relevant operational unit.

Phase 3: Advanced Specialization & Leadership

Objective: Develop advanced skills in a chosen specialty (e.g., Forensics, Cloud, Offensive Cyber) and prepare for leadership roles.

Advanced Certification Options:

  • **GIAC GCIH / GCIA:** Highly recommended for advanced incident handling and intrusion analysis.
  • **CISSP:** Essential for mid-career professionals moving into management and strategic decision-making.
  • **Elective Training:** Specialized vendor courses (e.g., Cisco, Splunk, specific cloud provider certifications).

Continuous Professional Development

The cyber domain changes constantly. Professionals must allocate time for continuous learning, attending conferences, and contributing to open-source projects to maintain currency.
